What changed? /

Investment research has always been programming.

Spreadsheets became the interface.

Copilot answers questions about spreadsheets.

fin123 answers questions about your firm's investment work.

fin123 adds the operating system around the model.

Search

Start with the model.

Search is the front door. Open a company, model, question, or research surface without managing files.

Search opens the current model and the research surfaces around it.
Analyze

Work in the model.

Keep the familiar grid and familiar formulas, but attach assumptions, outputs, evidence, formulas, and reasoning to the model itself.

The spreadsheet stays familiar. The context becomes durable.

Analyze is where model work, assumptions, outputs, and evidence stay in one surface.
Research Shell

Ask the model.

Research Shell understands how investment analysts work.

  • Why?
  • What changed?
  • What stands out?
  • Have we believed this before?
  • Remember this.
  • What if margin were 5%?
  • Write the report.

The shell speaks in the natural questions, memory checks, scenario tests, and writing tasks that already shape analyst work.

Analyst language covers asking, remembering, comparing, testing, and writing from the model.
Report

Turn work into communication.

Reports are emitted from model work. The explanation stays attached to the assumptions, outputs, evidence, and changes that produced it.

Report turns the model state into communication without detaching it from its evidence.
Timeline

Preserve what changed.

The timeline keeps institutional memory: what changed, when it changed, and why it mattered.

Timeline records versions, reports, and memory as the model evolves.
