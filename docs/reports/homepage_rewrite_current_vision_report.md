# Homepage Rewrite Current Vision Report

## What changed

Rewrote `index.html` around a more aspirational category frame: the future of investment research is not better spreadsheets, it is better thinking, and fin123 is the investment research operating system.

The homepage now leads with identity and desire rather than doctrine. It positions modern investment teams as teams that maintain living Models, then uses Model, Explore, Methods, investment language, headless research, and emitted records to support that claim.

The section structure now emphasizes:

- Research maintenance as the later "why this exists" reveal, not the opening lecture.
- The Model as the durable, executable representation of the thesis.
- Explore as the place where ideas become convictions.
- Analyst language as the public interaction model.
- Methods as reusable firm reasoning and investment judgment.
- Headless overnight preparation with analyst review before commitment.
- Timeline, Memory, Report, Audit, Replay, Provenance, and Governance as emitted representations.
- A new "Why serious teams move faster" identity section contrasting reasoning vs. files, ideas vs. spreadsheets, Models vs. reports, and conviction vs. documentation.
- Bring Your Model, Analyze grid, `=DATA()`, `=AI()`, AI Formula, Method, Results, Timeline, Report, Replay, and Audit as supporting product reality.

## Which doctrine guided the rewrite

The rewrite used:

- `research_velocity_development_environment_top_plan.md`
- `investment_research_development_environment_doctrine.md`
- `analytical_thread_doctrine.md`
- `analytical_reference_doctrine.md`
- `composition_doctrine.md`

The strongest guidance came from the research velocity and investment research development environment doctrines: research velocity is the objective, the Model is the executable representation of an investment thesis, Method is reusable reasoning, Explore is the fast loop, and institutional artifacts are emitted from Model work.

The analytical thread doctrine guided the move away from artifact management and toward preserving reasoning across surfaces.

The analytical reference doctrine guided the "Think in investment language" section and the distinction between analyst language and proof/debugging details.

The composition doctrine guided the copy away from making the browser or any frontend projection authoritative.

## How governance was demoted to exhaust

The old homepage made "Decision Trail" and governance-style proof a dominant product hook. The new copy removes governance from the primary nav and hero story.

Governance now appears in the "Everything else follows" and footer language as an emitted representation of reasoning. The page says explicitly: "Governance is exhaust, not the product. The desired user experience is: I am improving the Model."

Audit, Replay, provenance, and governance are framed as proof-on-demand for trust, review, diagnostics, and oversight.

## How Explore was introduced

Explore now has its own section framed as "where ideas become convictions":

`Idea -> Compare -> Judgment`

The public copy uses the requested language:

- Compare alternatives.
- See what changed.
- Keep what matters.
- Walk away from the rest.

Explore is positioned as the analyst's canvas: fast enough for real thinking, structured enough that evidence, comparison, Results, Timeline, Replay, and Audit remain available as proof-on-demand.

## How Methods were repositioned

Methods were moved away from "AI Method as Formula configuration" and into "how your firm thinks."

The new Methods section uses the stronger public claim: "Your Models are assets. Your Methods are your firm's intellectual property." It describes Method as reusable investment judgment and keeps AI Method / AI Formula present without making Method an AI configuration concept.

## How headless/overnight model updates were introduced

The headless section now uses the more memorable claim: "Your research should not sleep because you did." It introduces scheduled and overnight headless runs as preparation:

- refreshed Models
- new evidence
- changed assumptions
- attention items
- prepared Model updates

The section explicitly preserves authority: "The analyst still decides what to keep. Overnight preparation is not hidden canonical mutation and not auto-promotion."

- The page still uses existing screenshots, so future screenshots may need to better reflect Explore, overnight attention, and Model-centered reasoning.
